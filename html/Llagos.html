History of the Parrish

The history of Llagos is framed in the context of the aboriginal peoples of the Ecuadorian Andes, formerly and with complete certainty our parish territory was populated by some aboriginal group with a defined culture. with customs and traditions of that time, this can be affirmed with the variety of archaeological remains found, carved in clay such as pots, glasses, and stone in the figure of animals and humans, remains that thanks to studies have managed to locate three cultures chronologically located in the parish; The Tardío Narrio culture, which is said to be where the Cañarí culture originates from, has also found the presence of the Cañarí culture, and the Inca culture, some of which have aroused special interest in the competent organisms such as a historical center of studies and projection to attract tourism. The origin of the name of Llagos inherited by our land has some versions. -One of the versions mentions that it comes from the name of an aboriginal group that populated these lands, known as the LLAPOS, or ILAPOS. -Others think that it comes from the presence of Lakes or Lagoons, which existed at that time, of which only vestiges remain today. Whatever the origin of the name of Llagos, it is used for the first time from 1910, when by Public Deed Granted to Colonel Mancheno as the sole owner of an extensive hacienda that encompassed all the lands where Llagos and its communities are located today. Over time, these lavish lands were populated, mostly by families that migrated from the southern part of the country, especially from Paute and Gualaceo, etc. The same ones who worked as pawns or supporters and overtime were acquiring small parcels as purchases, this is how this large latifundium was parceled out and the families multiplied.
